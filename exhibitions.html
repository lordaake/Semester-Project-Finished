Explore Our 6 
 magical
exhibitions:

Cosmology

Explore the wonders of our cosmos. Our fantastic exhibition, ‘The Sky Above Us’, explores the night sky and what we can see and know about the universe around us. We’ll locate the various constellations and galaxies that can be seen and learn a bit about the early navigators who used the stars to travel by. Follow the journey of our solar exploration: from early Arab traders, to Galileo’s telescope, to the latest exploration of the planets in our solar system.

Robotics and AI

The Robotics and Artificial Intelligence exhibition will showcase the latest advancements in these exciting and rapidly developing fields. Children will have the opportunity to explore the various applications of AI and robotics, from their use in everyday household items to their role in industry and beyond. Visitors will be able to see, touch, and interact with some of the most advanced robots and AI systems in the world, providing a hands-on understanding of how these technologies work and their potential uses in the future.

Evolution

For centuries, philosophers and scientists have wrestled with the question of our origins. Where do we come from and how did we get here? Since Darwin proposed his theory of evolution we have had a framework for exploring and understanding our place in this world. Discover the origins of life on this planet and how the species we know today have evolved our time.

Ecology

As we learn more and more of the impact humans are having on the planet, the more important it becomes that scientists explore how to create a sustainable future not just for humans but for the whole planet. In our ecology section of the museum, we unpack the dangers we currently face including global warming, extinction of species, and pollution. But we also focus on the positive ways we can all contribute to a brighter future.

The Art Studio

This exhibition will give children a chance to explore their creative side through hands-on art activities, such as painting, drawing, and sculpting. Children can learn about different art styles and techniques, and create their own works of art to take home. The exhibition will feature a variety of materials, such as paints, markers, pencils, clay, and more, allowing children to experiment with different media and techniques. Children will be encouraged to try out new skills and techniques and to take risks in their artistic expression.

Dinosaurs

The World of Dinosaurs is an educational and interactive exhibition that transports children back in time to the era of the dinosaurs. With life-size models, fossils, and hands-on displays, children can learn about different dinosaur species and their habitats. Visitors will have the chance to touch and inspect real fossils, learn about the various types of dinosaurs that roamed the earth, and discover how they lived and hunted.
