Events and Exhibitions
For kids:

Are you a young person looking to learn more about science? Come on down to our museum, there’s plenty to see and do.

Our exhibits are designed to be accessible for interested minds, so make sure you come ready to learn and explore. We also have the Young Stars club which meets once a week on a Saturday between 10:00 and 13:00 where you’ll get to explore and experiment with our team of experts.

During the school holidays we run special holiday clubs where you can join other children your age to go on a journey of discovery. Each holiday we pick a new theme to explore. To find out more about the holiday club and how you can join, send us a message.

For teachers:

As a community-driven museum, we want to work with schools to create places of learning and exploring. Our team are on hand to give your students guided tours of the museum, teach them in our learning laboratory, and provide great video presentations that will excite and inspire them.

We understand that science education is important for young people and that many schools are on tight budgets.

Please get in touch with us here to find out more about our facilities and to arrange a time to bring your students through for a visit. We can't wait to share the wonder of science with your students!

Researchers:

Are you looking to get involved with our team of researchers and academics?

Whether you are a student looking to gain research experience or a professional researcher seeking access to our laboratory space, we can provide you with the tools and resources you need to make progress on your work.

We also have established relationships with a number of universities around the country, which means we can offer unique opportunities to work with other researchers and to participate in cutting-edge research projects.

If you are interested in getting involved with our team of researchers and academics, please contact us to learn more about the opportunities available.
